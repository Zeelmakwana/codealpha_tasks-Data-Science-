Analysis on "Unemployment in India.csv"

Observations:-

  1. The histogram shows that most unemployment rate values are concentrated between 3% and 15%.
     Very few observations have unemployment rates above 30%.

  2. States with relatively HIGH unemployment rates:
       • Tripura
       • Haryana
       • Jharkhand
       • Bihar
       • Himachal Pradesh

     States with comparatively LOW unemployment rates:
       • Assam
       • Gujarat
       • Andhra Pradesh
       • Karnataka
       • Meghalaya

     Different states have different economic structures, industries, and employment
     opportunities, which leads to variations in unemployment levels.

  3. An unemployment rate of nearly 18% during the Covid period indicates a substantial
     rise in job losses and economic disruption.

  4. The monthly trend graph shows a spike around April 2020, meaning the lockdown period
     had the most severe impact on employment.

  5. Top states with the highest average unemployment rates:
       1.  Tripura
       2.  Haryana
       3.  Jharkhand
       4.  Bihar
       5.  Himachal Pradesh
       6.  Delhi
       7.  Jammu & Kashmir
       8.  Chandigarh
       9.  Rajasthan
       10. Uttar Pradesh

  6. The average unemployment rate spiked from approximately 9.5% before Covid to 17.8%
     after Covid, demonstrating the economic impact of the pandemic.

Analysis on "Unemployment_Rate_upto_11_2020.csv"

Observations:-

     better employment stability during the Covid period.

       Region          Average Unemployment Rate (%)
       ---------       -----------------------------
       North           15.89%
       East            13.92%
       Northeast       10.95%
       South           10.45%
       West             8.24%

     The East and West regions had the highest employment levels, while the Northeast
     region had the lowest employment figures.

       Region          Average Employment
